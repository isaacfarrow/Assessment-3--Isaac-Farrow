Waitangi Day

Every year on 6 February, New Zealand marks the signing of the Treaty of Waitangi in 1840. In that year, representatives of the British Crown and over 500 MÄori chiefs signed what is often considered to be New Zealandâ€™s founding document. The day was first officially commemorated in 1934, and it has been a public holiday since 1974.
